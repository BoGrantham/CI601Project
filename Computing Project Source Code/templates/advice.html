Understanding Neurodiversity

Learn about different neurodivergent conditions and how to support them.

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

ASD affects social interaction, communication, interests, and behavior. It varies widely among individuals.

  • Use clear, literal language when communicating.
  • Provide structured routines and expectations.
  • Allow time for processing information.

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

ADHD can cause inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsiveness, affecting work and social interactions.

  • Break tasks into manageable chunks.
  • Use reminders and visual schedules.
  • Provide movement breaks when needed.

Dyslexia

Dyslexia primarily affects reading, spelling, and writing skills due to differences in how the brain processes language.

  • Use text-to-speech tools and audiobooks.
  • Present information visually when possible.
  • Allow extra time for reading-based tasks.

Dyspraxia

Dyspraxia affects physical coordination and can make everyday tasks more difficult.

  • Offer tools for fine motor support (e.g., ergonomic pens).
  • Give step-by-step instructions for physical tasks.
  • Be patient and supportive with movement-based activities.

Dyscalculia

Dyscalculia involves difficulty with math concepts, numbers, and reasoning.

  • Use concrete examples and visual aids.
  • Allow calculator use and written formulas.
  • Break down math problems into clear steps.

  • Do not draw attention to tics.
  • Provide quiet spaces if needed.
  • Promote understanding and acceptance.

Sensory Processing Disorder (SPD)

SPD affects how the brain processes sensory information, causing over- or under-responsiveness.

  • Offer sensory-friendly environments.
  • Provide fidget tools or sensory breaks.
  • Respect sensory boundaries.

Nonverbal Learning Disorder (NVLD)

NVLD involves challenges with visual-spatial and nonverbal cues, despite strong verbal skills.

  • Use clear instructions with visuals.
  • Offer support for social cues and body language.
  • Provide structured routines.
